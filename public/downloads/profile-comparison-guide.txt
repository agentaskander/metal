AMERICAS PANEL FAB
PROFILE COMPARISON GUIDE

Use this guide to compare common metal roof and wall panel profile families
before requesting pricing, drawings, or project review.

Common profile families:

Standing seam
- Concealed-fastener roof system conversation.
- Often used where appearance, long-term roof planning, and commercial review matter.
- Confirm slope, substrate, panel width, seam type, clips/fasteners, finish, and warranty path.

Exposed fastener / ribbed panels
- Practical roof and siding panel conversation for commercial, industrial, agricultural,
  warehouse, and utility buildings.
- Confirm rib shape, coverage width, side lap, fastener path, gauge, substrate, finish,
  trim, closures, and roof or wall use.

Wall panels and siding panels
- Used for exterior walls, re-skins, commercial shells, shops, and architectural accents.
- Confirm orientation, openings, corners, base trim, cap trim, color breaks, and finish.

Trim, flashing, and accessories
- Ridge, rake, eave, corner, jamb, base, transition, cap, closures, fasteners, and
  accessory scope should be reviewed with the panel package.

Comparison checklist:
- Building use
- Roof, wall, or mixed scope
- Profile drawing and coverage width
- Fastener method
- Gauge and substrate path
- Finish and color path
- Trim and flashing scope
- Closures and accessories
- Delivery state and schedule target
- Submittal or owner approval requirements

Public note:
Final product dimensions, engineering, code, warranty, wind, fire, and installation
requirements must be confirmed through project-specific review and current
manufacturer documentation.
