Americas Panel Fab
Roll Forming Workflow One-Pager

Purpose:
This public worksheet explains the typical information flow behind a metal panel
roll-forming conversation. It is not engineering, installation, warranty, code,
or project-specific production approval.

1. Project intake
- Project name and state
- Contractor, owner, or buyer contact
- Roof, wall, trim, or mixed scope
- Drawings, photos, sketches, or approximate dimensions
- Desired schedule or delivery window

2. Product path review
- Panel profile or profile family
- Roof use, wall use, or combined use
- Approximate square footage
- Desired panel lengths, if known
- Trim, flashing, closure, and accessory needs

3. Material and finish review
- Gauge preference, if known
- Substrate path, if specified
- Finish family or color preference
- Sample, submittal, or owner approval needs
- Any exposure notes such as coastal, high-sun, agricultural, or industrial use

4. Roll-forming coordination
- Profile drawing and coverage width should be confirmed before ordering
- Panel lengths should be reviewed for handling, delivery, and jobsite access
- Finish, gauge, substrate, and color assumptions should be visible in the quote
- Trim and flashing should be coordinated with the panel package

5. Package handoff
- Panels counted and organized by project area when possible
- Bundles labeled clearly for receiving and field use
- Trim, flashing, fasteners, closures, and accessories reviewed as separate scope
- Delivery contact, address, timing, and site constraints confirmed

Public-safe note:
Final project requirements, equipment limits, code requirements, wind/fire
requirements, engineering review, warranty terms, and installation details must be
confirmed through qualified review and manufacturer documentation.
