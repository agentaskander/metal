AMERICAN SUPER PANEL
COLOR CHART & SAMPLE REQUEST GUIDE

Use this guide to organize color, finish, sample, trim, and flashing decisions
before ordering metal roofing or siding panels.

Named color paths:

Galvalume
- Metallic utility finish path.
- Common for roof, siding, trim, shops, warehouses, and agricultural packages.

Galvanized
- Bright metallic utility look.
- Confirm substrate, coating, finish expectations, and project use.

Rezibond-Style Primer-Ready
- Weathered zinc / gunmetal design intent.
- Confirm primer-ready availability, substrate, and final use before release.

Polar White
- Light cool-roof color path.
- Useful for heat-aware projects and trim packages.

Light Stone
- Practical tan neutral.
- Common for barns, shops, storage buildings, and commercial work.

Slate Gray
- Commercial gray path.
- Useful for warehouses, shops, trim, siding, and owner-standard projects.

Charcoal
- Modern dark gray path.
- Useful for siding, trim, fascia, and commercial accents.

Matte Black
- Low-glare architectural dark finish.
- Sample review recommended before visible orders.

Burnished Slate
- Dark earth-tone path.
- Useful for agricultural, commercial, and storage buildings.

Rustic Red
- Traditional agricultural and accent path.
- Sample review recommended.

Forest Green
- Rural, agricultural, and owner-standard green path.
- Confirm availability by panel type and finish family.

Copper Penny
- Specialty accent path.
- Best reviewed for trim, fascia, soffit, and visible architectural details.

Sample request checklist:
- Project name and state
- Roof, wall, trim, or flashing use
- Desired panel profile
- Desired color names
- Finish family preference, if known
- Gauge or substrate preference, if known
- Owner, architect, or contractor approval needs
- Timeline for sample review

Important note:
Screen colors are planning references only. Final color, coating system,
substrate, gauge, trim match, warranty path, and availability must be confirmed
with current samples and project-specific review before release.
